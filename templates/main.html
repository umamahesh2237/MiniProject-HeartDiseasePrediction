Understanding the Input Features

To help us predict your risk of heart disease, please provide the following information from your medical records. If you don't have a recent record for a specific value, consult your doctor.

  • Age (Years): Your current age.
  • Sex: Male or Female.
  • Chest Pain Type (cp):
    • Value 0: Typical angina (chest pain that feels like pressure or squeezing)
    • Value 1: Atypical angina (chest pain that doesn't have the typical features of angina)
    • Value 2: Non-anginal pain (chest pain that isn't caused by heart problems)
    • Value 3: Asymptomatic (no chest pain)

    Hint: This information might be noted in a doctor's report or from discussions about chest discomfort.

  • Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps): Your blood pressure reading in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) while at rest.

    Hint: Look for blood pressure readings in your medical records or from a recent home blood pressure measurement.

  • Serum Cholesterol (chol): Your total cholesterol level in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL).

    Hint: This value is usually found in a blood test report.

  • Fasting Blood Sugar (> 120 mg/dL) (fbs):
    • Value 0: Fasting blood sugar less than or equal to 120 mg/dL (normal)
    • Value 1: Fasting blood sugar greater than 120 mg/dL (possibly diabetic)

    Hint: Check blood test reports for fasting blood sugar levels.

  • Resting Electrocardiographic Results (restecg):
    • Value 0: Normal resting ECG
    • Value 1: ST-T wave abnormality (may indicate heart damage)
    • Value 2: Probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy (enlarged heart muscle)

    Hint: This information might be in an ECG report or discussed by your doctor.

  • Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach): The highest heart rate you reached during exercise testing, measured in beats per minute (bpm).

    Hint: This value is usually found in an exercise stress test report.

  • Exercise-Induced Angina (exang):
    • Value 0: No
    • Value 1: Yes

    Hint: Indicate if you experience chest pain during physical activity.

  • ST Depression Induced by Exercise Relative to Rest (oldpeak): The amount of ST depression (a finding on the ECG) that occurs during exercise compared to when at rest.

    Hint: This value might be in an exercise stress test report or discussed by your doctor.

  • Slope of the Peak Exercise ST Segment (slope):
    • Value 0: Upsloping (typically normal)
    • Value 1: Flat (may indicate heart disease)
    • Value 2: Downsloping (more indicative of heart disease)

    Hint: This value is often noted in an exercise stress test report.

  • Number of Major Vessels Colored by Fluoroscopy (ca): The number of major vessels (0-3) showing significant narrowing.

    Hint: Look for this information in a coronary angiogram report.

  • Thalassemia (thal):
    • Value 1: Normal blood flow
    • Value 2: Fixed defect (permanent damage)
    • Value 3: Reversible defect (temporary reduction in blood flow)

    Hint: This information might be found in a thalassemia screening report or discussed by your doctor.

If you have any questions or need assistance, please consult with your healthcare provider for the most accurate and up-to-date information.
